§ 02 / Reference

From legacy ERP to cloud architecture.

The client — a leading sports retailer — had been running a heavily customized ERP that had absorbed all business logic over the years. The sheer level of customization made upgrades virtually impossible, and a lot of critical knowledge lived only in people's heads.

The strategic call: go all-in on SAP — with the latest products across backend and frontend. To roll out Order Management Foundation, OMSA, Integration Suite, and S/4HANA without unnecessary risk, we connected the new frontend to the legacy backend first, then swapped out the old systems piece by piece. The result: a smooth, controlled transition.

In parallel, we pulled business logic out of the ERP and moved it into the cloud layer — building towards a lean, maintainable clean core. Today, both the approach and the architecture are recognised as an SAP reference, showcased at the DSAG annual conference and ESUG Onsite.

01 / 05

Order Capture

Orders can arrive from any channel — webshop, app, or marketplace. Sourcing runs through OMSA, and payment authorisation goes straight from the channel to the PSP. Once that's done, the channel passes the order to OMF — and if sourcing is missing, OMF picks it up.

02 / 05

Customer Communication

OMF centrally drives all CX touchpoints: order emails like confirmations go through Emarsys, profile data flows to the CDP. There's only one place where updates happen — so new sales channels just plug into OMF instead of building their own integrations.

03 / 05

Orchestration

OMF uses configurable rules to decide which ERP handles an order. That makes the whole setup extremely flexible — you can swap or add ERPs without touching anything around them.

04 / 05

Status Updates

Every lifecycle event flows back into OMF: shipment status from ParcelLab, payment status from the PSP, plus ERP events. OMF becomes the single source of truth. Customer notifications go out through Emarsys, updates feed the CDP — and anything that goes wrong becomes a ticket in the Service Cloud.

05 / 05

Shipping & Returns

ParcelLab handles shipment tracking and return registration. OMF creates the return order in the right ERP — and once the credit note is posted, triggers the refund through the PSP.

Benefits of the Architecture
FIELD_02
01

Clean Core — get logic out of the ERP

Business logic moves out of the ERP and into cloud services. The ERP stays lean — far easier to maintain and ready for upgrades.

02

Plug in new channels, fast

A new webshop, app, or marketplace just connects to OMF — everything downstream is already wired up. Onboarding a new channel becomes trivial.

03

Swap ERPs without breaking things

With OMF holding the orchestration logic, ERPs become interchangeable. Need to add a dropshipper or migrate to a new ERP? No problem.

04

Full order visibility in one place

An order-to-refund flow touches many systems — some you don't even control, like a dropshipper's ERP. Keeping a full picture of every order is hard but essential: for customer support and a consistent CX. OMF collects updates from ERPs, carriers, and PSPs alike, giving you complete order visibility in a single place.
